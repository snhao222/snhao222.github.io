I. Introduction

An army of applications on UAVs rely on deep learning (computing intensive, no military application) , ðŸ‘‡

UAVs usually have limited computing, memory and power resource ðŸ‘‡

Many works have paid attention to leverage the external powerful servers such as cloud and edge to reduce the burden of resource-constrained end devices ( i.e., Cloud-Device CoEI, Edge-Device CoEI, Cloud-Edge-Device CoEI, Device-Device CoEI). ðŸ‘‡

Limitation for three conventional CoEI paradigms: communication link between UAVs and remote cloud server or ground edge is fluctuant, air-to-air links in a UAV swarm are unreliable and UAVs may breakdown for hardware/software errors. ðŸ‘‡

This article presents E-CoEI( Elastic Collaborative Edge Intelligence), a brand new architecture which enables a UAV swarm to collaboratively perform a DNN inference task in an on-demand and flexible manner.ðŸ‘‡

The main contributions of this work:

(1) Propose a new CoEI framework, which could well adapt to UAV swarms with high dynamics;

(2) We construct a CoEI system and conduct a proof-of concept evaluation on E-CoEI framework, the results show that our proposed architecture is feasible and effective for Device-Device CoEI, even if the communication links and node conditions are unreliable;

(3) We point out the main technical challenges in the E-CoEI framework, as well as potential research directions based on E-CoEI for future study.

VI. Conclusion

This article introduced E-CoEI, an invulnerable distributed COEI architecture focus on unstable A2A communication links and UAVs software/hardware breakdown. The preliminary evaluation over the self-built airborne embedded system demonstrated the effectiveness and feasibility of our proposed E-CoEI framework. There still lies a couple of technical challenges to realize its broad vision, such as UAV swarm status discovery, inference task distribution, etc. At the same time, there are promising research directions including UAV swarm management, network protocols design, multiple collaborative inference flows scheduling, dedicated DNN structure design, etc.
